Cheick Ismaël Tioté

The box-to-box midfielder

Cheikh Tioté clapping playing for Newcastle as captain in 2013

Here is the time line of Tioté’s life:

  • 1986 - Born in Yamousoukro, Ivory, Coast.
  • 2001- Begin his football career with FC Bibo
  • 2005-Signed three years contract with Belgian side Anderlecht.
  • 2007-Loan to Roda JC for a season long loan where he played alongside his countryman Sekou Cisse
  • 2008-Signed for Dutch Eredivisie side, FC Twente for a fee around €750,000. Signing four-year contract.
  • 2009-Called up by the Ivory Coast national team for the first time but did not play. Months later he made his international debut in a friendly match against Tunisia.
  • 2010-Joined English premier league side Newcastle United for fee of £3.5 million and given number 24 shirt.
  • 2011-Tioté’s car was seized by police for monitoring offences.
  • 2012-Sent off for a crazy challenge in an African Cup of Nation wamp up match against Tunisia.
  • 2013-Captained Newcastle for the first time replacing Fabricio Coloccini and Yohan Cabaye both of whom were ruled out by injury.
  • 2014-Tioté married a second wife, Laeticia Doukrou, in the capital of Ivory Coast, Abidjan. The marriage take place before the start of the season.
  • 2015-Sustained injury that ruled him out of 2015 African Cup of Nations.
  • 2017-Tioté joined China league one side Beijing Enterprises for an undisclosed fee.
  • 2017-Tioté died in Beijing, China, after suffering from probable cardiac arrest in training.

"Growing up in Abdijan, I knew what I wanted to do and made sure that this was going to be my life. But I worked and worked and worked for it and it is because of that hard work that I have managed to make it"

- Cheick Tioté
